-- Wedding Day Rituals 😊


## Sehra Bandi 👑

Before leaving with the baraat, the groom's family ties a beautifully decorated sehra (floral veil) on his turban. It is believed to protect him from the evil eye, bring blessings, and symbolize dignity as he begins his wedding journey.


## Baraat 🐎

The Baraat is the grand wedding procession in which the groom travels to the wedding venue with his family and friends. Traditionally, he rides a decorated horse, although many modern weddings feature luxury cars or themed entries. The procession is filled with lively music, energetic dancing, and celebrations, creating a joyful atmosphere. It represents the groom's happiness as he arrives to marry his bride and marks the coming together of two families for a new beginning.


## Aarti & Tilak 🪔

The bride's mother welcomes the groom by performing an aarti and applying a tilak on his forehead before he enters the venue. This ritual is performed to bless the groom, protect him from negative energy, and warmly welcome him into the family.


## Nose Pulling Ritual 👃 (Popular in North India)

n many North Indian weddings, the bride's mother playfully pulls the groom's nose after the welcome ceremony. This light-hearted tradition reminds him to stay humble, respect his new family, and always value his relationship with the bride.


## Jaimala (Varmala) 🌺

The Jaimala Ceremony is one of the first wedding rituals where the bride and groom exchange flower garlands in front of their families and guests. This beautiful tradition symbolizes their acceptance, love, respect, and willingness to begin a lifelong journey together.


## Kanyadaan 🔥

Kanyadaan is one of the most sacred rituals in a Hindu wedding. The bride's parents place their daughter's hand into the groom's hand while offering prayers and blessings. This emotional ceremony symbolizes their trust, love, and blessings as the bride begins a new chapter of her life.


## Phere 🔥

During the Saat Phere, the bride and groom take seven rounds around the sacred fire while making seven vows to support, respect, and stand by each other throughout life. The sacred fire acts as a witness, making these promises eternal.

Interesting Fact

In many Sindhi weddings, the bride and groom take four sacred pheras around the holy fire instead of seven. Each phera represents love, trust, responsibility, and lifelong commitment. This sacred ritual marks the beginning of their new journey together as husband and wife.


## Sindoor Ceremony ❤️

After the Saat Phere, the groom applies sindoor to the bride's hair parting. In many Hindu traditions, this ritual symbolizes marriage, lifelong commitment, and the beginning of the bride's new life as a married woman.

Interesting Fact

According to a popular traditional belief, if the groom accidentally applies sindoor on the bride's nose while filling her hair parting, it is considered a sign of deep love and affection. Although this is only a cultural belief and not a proven fact, many families see it as a sweet and auspicious moment during the wedding ceremony.


## Mangalsutra Ceremony 📿

The groom ties the mangalsutra around the bride's neck as a symbol of their lifelong bond. This sacred necklace represents love, trust, protection, and the promise of staying together through every stage of life.
