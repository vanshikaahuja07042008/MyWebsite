-- Pre-Wedding Rituals 🌼


## Ganesh Puja 🙏

The Ganesh Puja is one of the first rituals performed before the wedding celebrations begin. Family members gather to offer prayers to Lord Ganesha, seeking his blessings for a smooth and joyful wedding. The priest performs the puja with flowers, incense, sweets, and sacred chants, creating a peaceful and spiritual atmosphere.Lord Ganesha is worshipped as the remover of obstacles. This ritual is believed to bring good fortune, positivity, and blessings for a happy married life.


## Roka Ceremony 🤝

The Roka Ceremony marks the official beginning of the wedding journey. It is the first formal occasion where both families meet, accept the relationship, and bless the bride and groom. Families exchange sweets, gifts, flowers, and warm wishes while celebrating the start of a beautiful new chapter together.The Roka Ceremony symbolizes acceptance, unity, and the coming together of two families. It officially announces the couple's journey towards marriage.


## Engagement (Sagai) 💍

The Engagement Ceremony, also known as Sagai, is a joyful celebration where the bride and groom officially announce their commitment to each other by exchanging rings. Surrounded by family and friends, the couple receives blessings while enjoying music, celebrations, and the exchange of gifts.The exchange of rings symbolizes love, trust, loyalty, and the promise of a lifelong partnership. It marks the beginning of the couple's official commitment before marriage.


## Haldi Ceremony 💛

The Haldi Ceremony is one of the most vibrant and joyful pre-wedding rituals in an Indian wedding. During this ceremony, a paste made from turmeric, sandalwood, and rose water or milk is gently applied to the bride and groom by their family and friends. It is filled with laughter, music, and cheerful celebrations.

The bright yellow color of turmeric represents happiness, positivity, and prosperity. Along with its traditional importance, turmeric is also known for its natural skincare benefits, helping to cleanse and brighten the skin before the wedding. The ceremony creates a fun and memorable atmosphere as loved ones come together to celebrate the couple.It symbolizes purification, protection, and good fortune.

Interesting Fact

After the Haldi ceremony, many families follow a tradition where the bride and groom do not meet or see each other until the wedding ceremony. In some communities, they also avoid going outside after Haldi. This custom is believed to bring good luck and make the couple's first meeting on the wedding day more special.


## Mehendi Ceremony 🌿

The Mehendi Ceremony is one of the most colorful and exciting pre-wedding celebrations. Beautiful henna designs are applied to the bride's hands and feet, while family members and friends also enjoy decorating their hands with mehendi. The event is filled with music, dance, fun, and joyful celebrations.

Many people also believe that the darker the mehendi color, the stronger the love and affection between the couple, although this is a traditional belief.The Mehendi Ceremony symbolizes love, beauty, joy, prosperity, and good fortune. It marks the beginning of a happy new phase in the bride's life and is considered one of the most cherished wedding traditions.

Interesting Fact

According to a popular Indian wedding belief, the darker the bride's mehendi color, the deeper the love and affection between the couple. It is also believed to reflect the love and care of the bride's new family. Although this is a traditional belief and not a proven fact, it adds joy and excitement to the Mehendi ceremony.


## Sangeet Ceremony 🎶

The Sangeet Ceremony is a lively celebration where both families come together to enjoy music, dance, and entertainment before the wedding. Friends and relatives perform choreographed dances, sing songs, and celebrate the couple in a joyful and festive atmosphere.

The evening is filled with laughter, performances, games, and unforgettable moments. It is a wonderful opportunity for both families to bond, celebrate together, and create lasting memories before the wedding day.It symbolizes happiness, togetherness, and the joyful union of two families. It creates a relaxed and festive atmosphere before the sacred wedding rituals begin.


## Chooda Ceremony (Popular in Punjabi Weddings) ❤️

The Chooda Ceremony is a cherished pre-wedding ritual in many Punjabi families. The bride's maternal uncle and aunt present her with a set of red and ivory bangles after performing traditional prayers and blessings. In many families, the bride keeps her eyes closed or does not look at the chooda before wearing it, as it is considered an auspicious tradition.

The bride wears the chooda as a symbol of her new journey into married life. The duration for wearing it varies according to family traditions and customs.It symbolizes blessings, happiness, prosperity, and the beginning of the bride's new life as a married woman.


## Kaleera Ceremony (Popular in Punjabi Weddings) ✨

The Kaleera Ceremony takes place after the bride wears her chooda. Beautiful umbrella-shaped ornaments called kaleere are tied to the bride's bangles by her sisters, cousins, and close relatives as a symbol of love and blessings. The ceremony is filled with excitement and joyful moments.

One of the most popular traditions is when the bride gently shakes her kaleere over the heads of her unmarried friends and cousins. According to a popular belief, if a kaleera falls on someone, that person may be the next to get married. This is a fun family tradition and is enjoyed by everyone.
